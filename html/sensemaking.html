sensemaking

how to see clearly when the fog is manufactured — the capacity under every other skill.

"disinformation is pollution to the information ecology… if i want to be able to make sense of the world well, i have to work at that."

— daniel schmachtenberger, the war on sensemaking

most of what reaches you was built to move you, not to inform you. nearly every signal is strategic before it is honest. the way out is not a better feed — it is your own capacity, grown on purpose. six practices, from daniel's work:

🕯️earnestness want the truth more than the win⌄

a disposition before a technique: wanting the truth more than the win, more than your past self being right, more than the conversation feeling good. without it, every tool below just sharpens your bias.

try: once a day, catch yourself mid-certainty and ask — how would i know if i were wrong? if nothing could change your mind, you're not sensing. you're defending.

more

daniel's version of the bad news: "everybody wants to be buff, but nobody wants to lift heavy-ass weights." attention and nuance are trainable. shallow stimuli untrain both.

🧾provenance how far from the primary data?⌄

every claim arrives through a chain of custody — measured, summarized, headlined, screenshotted. distortion compounds at each link, so certainty should shrink with distance. mostly it doesn't.

try: take one claim you shared this week and walk the chain backward. can you find the primary source? what happened to its caveats on the way to you?

more

daniel's question for anything secondhand: "how many steps removed is it, and how good is the original data?" screenshot-of-a-headline beliefs deserve screenshot-of-a-headline confidence.

🎭the three lies commission, omission, emphasis⌄

only the first one looks like lying:

  • commission — saying what's false. the rarest; the easiest to catch.
  • omission — saying only the true things that help. the story dies in what went unsaid.
  • emphasis — every fact true, every proportion wrong.

try: next outrage headline — before "is this true?", ask: what would the whole picture need, and why isn't it here?

more

past all three: information can be true, truthfully shared, and still unrepresentative. when what gets funded, studied, and amplified is skewed, honest fragments assemble into a dishonest whole.

🌿many hypotheses hold several stories, loosely⌄

the fog wants you to collapse early — pick a story, join its army. keep two or three alive instead, each carrying the steelman its smartest believer would sign.

try: take a belief you'd defend at dinner. write the other side well enough that you'd be embarrassed to lose to it. if you can't, you don't understand your own position yet.

more

most "paradoxes" are two partial lenses arguing. daniel's method is holding several at once until a higher-dimensional picture resolves.

🎣the hook what does this message want from you?⌄

the fastest check isn't about the message — it's about you. strong feeling arriving with information is usually the delivery mechanism. if it hooks, someone built the hook.

try: when something demands to be shared immediately, that urgency is the tell. ten breaths. notice what the message wanted your body to do.

more

cui bono: attention converts to money, and your rage is the crop — babel x-rays the machine that farms it.

🕸️together no one makes sense of this alone⌄

daniel's most concrete instruction: "start to create relationships where one of the highest values is truthfulness… where people are not only not lying to each other, but endeavouring to not withhold information."

try: name the two or three people you'd trust to say you're fooling yourself. then be that person for someone else.

more

the world's complexity exceeds any one mind. find people who disagree in good faith and update out loud — the ecology heals one honest relationship at a time.
