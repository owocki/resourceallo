🔤five words, one school

c

consilience

the jumping-together of knowledge. whewell coined it in 1840; e.o. wilson revived it. when the archaeology, the economics, and the folklore all point the same way — you're allowed to start believing.

r

research

asking questions that outlive their answers, then checking. twelve thousand years of receipts: who got what, who decided, what it cost, what it became.

o

ontology

a map of what exists and how it hangs together. ours currently holds 1,063 mechanisms, ten civilizational questions, five eras, one coil.

w

wisdom

knowledge is knowing what's true. wisdom is knowing what matters, in what order, and what you owe the people downstream of you.

s

school

of thought. of fish. of crows teaching their young which faces to trust. a place to learn in public, together, out loud.

🐦‍⬛why the bird

the omen & the oracle

crows are double-booked in world folklore: dark omen in one story, bird of prophecy and creation in the next. raven steals the sun for the tlingit; two of them — thought and memory — perch on odin's shoulders and fly the world for news.

a school about civilization needs exactly that temperament: a bird comfortable reading both the wreckage and the dawn.

gatherers of shiny things

corvids use tools, solve multi-step puzzles, remember human faces for years, hold quiet vigils over their dead — and famously bring small bright gifts to people who feed them.

that is the whole method here: gather the shiny mechanisms, bring them back to the flock, and remember which ideas fed us and which ones threw stones.
